    Trade Account Markup Group and Rebates

    Willow (OB)
    April 16, 2026 04:01 Updated

    The article details rebate settings for different trading account types: Standard, ECN, Prime, and Cent, including their respective account codes, instruments, and rebate values. It highlights that some rebates require special approval and that Cent account rebates are divided by 100. The rebate scheme example shows hierarchical rebate limits for IB levels, emphasizing sub-IB rebates cannot exceed upper IBs.

    • Standard
    • ECN
    • Prime
    • Cent (Standard & Prime)

    To set up rebates, please refer to the following article: How to set rebate for IB in "Broker User"

    By default, all newly created trading accounts will have the symbol “S” at the end of their account group code. For example, when opening a Standard+2 account, the default account group would be STD_P20S. 
    If the code ends with “N”, for example STD_P20N, it means the account has activated the swap-free function.

    Standard Account Types

    *Forex Groups refers to following instruments: FX, Oil, Cryptocurrency (BTCUST and ETHUST)

    ECN Account Type

    Note - Currently we do not support Stock rebate in MT4 ECN.

    *Forex Groups refers to following instruments: FX, Oil, Cryptocurrency (BTCUST and ETHUST)

    *The rebate settings for ECN16 require approval from BD.

    Prime Account Type

    *Forex Groups refers to following instruments: FX, Oil, Cryptocurrency (BTCUST and ETHUST)

    **The rebate settings for Prime_Com3 & Prime_Com6 require approval from Justin.

    Cent Account Type

    For Cent Account, the rebate amount has been already divided by 100, as 1 USD=100 USC.

    *Forex Groups refers to following instruments: FX, Oil, Cryptocurrency (BTCUST and ETHUST)

     **The question mark (?) represents the account group letter assigned to different clients. The most common one is F. The following letters are currently in use: A, B, C, D, E, F, S, T

     

    *Before setting up the Cent markup rebate, we must clarify which team is making the request:

    1. Justin team is added for Standard+1 Cent, Standard+2 Cent, and Standard+3 Cent. This is case by case for each IB, which is mean AM, TL, RGM have to get special approval for MIB from Justin, and after the approval is got, the whole tree will be accepted.
    • Standard+1 Cent, Standard+2 Cent, and Standard+3 Cent 
    • Once the requests have been approved by BD/John Lee, Sub-IBs can apply for specific account type without the need for a further approval and they can operate with their accounts directly.
