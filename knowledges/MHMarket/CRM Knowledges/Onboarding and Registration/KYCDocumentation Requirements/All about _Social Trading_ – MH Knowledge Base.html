    What is Social Trading?

    Social trading is a form of investing that allows traders to copy, follow, or interact with other investors in real time. It combines social networking with financial trading, enabling less experienced traders to learn from and replicate the strategies of successful traders.

    Key Features of Social Trading:

    1. Copy Trading – Automatically replicate the trades of expert investors.

    2. Mirror Trading – Follow pre-set strategies (not necessarily tied to a specific trader).

    3. Community Interaction – Discuss strategies, share insights, and follow market sentiment.

    4. Transparency – Track performance history, risk levels, and past trades of top investors.

    5. Accessibility – Beginners can participate without deep market knowledge.

     

    Rules and Guidelines

    Deposit Amount and Account Type

    • Provider: Minimum deposit is 50 USD.
    • Follower: This account must meet the Provider's minimum deposit and the initial activation deposit for this account type.
    • Social Trading supports specific account types such as:
    1. STD: P00, P10, P20, P30,...
    2. Prime: P00, P05, P10, P15, P20, P25, P30,...
    3. ECN: P00, P01, P02, P03, P05, P10,...
    4. New Cent Accounts

    Type of Fees

    • Performance Fee with Provider: Up to 70%, paid by Follower for profitable trades according to Provider's successful strategy
    • Platform Fee: None
    • Registration Fee: None
    • Management Fee: None

    Who is the Provider?

    Provider account is a trading account which acts as a source for trading orders copied to the Follower's account, including the following functions:

    • Register as a new Provider via Social Trading.
    • Attract Followers: Achieve high rankings on the leaderboard or suggest attractive offers to Followers.
    • Manage settings: Configure and manage Provider-specific settings.
    • Trade: Execute trading orders via Meta Trader as usual.
    • Profit sharing: Earn from Followers based on profitable closing orders.

    Who is the Follower?

    Follower account is a customer's trading account that copies closing orders from the Provider's account, including the following functions:

    • Register to copy multiple Providers.
    • Search and register to copy Providers via the Ranking or via private link.
    • Manage copy closing orders.
    • Receive profits from copy closing orders that Provider closes profit.
    • Share profits with Providers according to the selected Offer.

    Performance Fee

    • Performance Fee (in percentage) of a Follower's profitable trading results for the Provider. Negative trading results will not be shared.
    • Performance Fee Period: Daily, Weekly, Monthly
    • Performance Fee Type: HWM (High Water Mark) including floating PnL

     

    High-Water Mark (HWM) Mode:

    The High-Water Mark mode (HWM) takes into account previous trading results within a subscription period. In this mode, a performance fee is only earned if the total trading results in the current subscription cover any previous losses and exceed the highest profit previously achieved (the High-Water Mark). Once a new High-Water Mark is reached, it is updated to this higher level.

    A new High-Water Mark can never be lower than the previous one, and it is always a positive value (starting from an initial value of 0). Performance fees are only paid for positive trading results, meaning providers are compensated only if their trades consistently generate profits for their followers. Losses are acceptable, but providers must first recover any losses before earning a performance fee again. Contrary to some misconceptions, providers are not required to surpass their previous highest result to earn a performance fee; the High-Water Mark is not a historical record but a current threshold.

    Example:

    • Performance Fee: 20%
    • Interval: Weekly
    • Trading Result Mode: HWM
    1. Week 1: PnL = $50 → $10 performance fee is paid (20% of $50), High-Water Mark is set to $50.
    2. Week 2: PnL = -$50 → No fee (Net result of $0, which does not exceed the $50 High-Water Mark).
    3. Week 3: PnL = $20 → No fee (Net result of $20, which is still below the $50 High-Water Mark).
    4. Week 4: PnL = $35 → $1 fee is paid (20% of the $5 profit exceeding the $50 High-Water Mark, updated to $55).
    5. Week 5: PnL = -$15 → No fee (Net result of $40, which is below the new $55 High-Water Mark).

    This process continues accordingly.
