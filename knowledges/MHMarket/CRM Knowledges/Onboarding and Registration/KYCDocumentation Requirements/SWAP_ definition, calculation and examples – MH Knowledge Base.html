    SWAP: definition, calculation and examples

    Eric
    January 30, 2026 13:09 Updated

    This article provides an overview of the structure, purpose, and mechanics of overnight interest rates - SWAPs. A swap fee - also known as an overnight fee, rollover fee, or financing charge—is the cost or credit applied when a trader holds a leveraged position open beyond the market’s daily cutoff time (typically overnight). 

    Swap fees can be positive or negative:

    • Positive Swap: The trader earns interest when the position aligns with favorable interest rate differentials.
    • Negative Swap: The trader pays interest when the position incurs financing costs due to the rate differential or broker funding charges.

    Definition: Triple Swap (in Trading)

    A triple swap (triple rollover) - is the application of three days’ worth of swap fees or credits on a single day. This occurs because financial markets must account for settlement dates that fall on weekends when positions cannot be rolled over.

    Why It Happens?

    In most markets (especially forex and CFDs), positions settle T+2 - two business days after the trade date. To ensure weekend interest is properly accounted for, brokers apply three days of swap charges or credits on one specific weekday.

    When It Happens?

    • Triple swap is usually applied on Wednesday.
    • From Friday to Monday is counted as one night

    The detailed three times overnight deduction day can also check in “Contract Specification” of each symbol.

    Overnight interest rate (in points)

    Formula: Point value per lot × Number of lots × overnight interest rate (long or short) × number of days

    Example 1:

    For example: buy order 1 lot of EURUSD on Monday and close the position on Friday. 

    SWAP = 1 × 1 × (-6.2568) (assume the interest rate not changing) × (1+1+3+1) = -$37.54

    Example 2:

    Place a sell order of USDCAD 0.5 lots on Tuesday, close the position on Friday.

    Example 3: 

    SWAP = 1 × 1 × (-6.2568) (assume the interest rate not changing) × (1+1+3+1) = -$37.540.73 (CAD to USD exchange rate) × 0.5 × (-6.0368) × (1+3+1) = -$11.02 

    Example 4:

    Place a sell order for 0.5 lots of XAUUSD on Monday and close the position on Thursday.

    SWAP = 1 × 0.5 × (14.2884) × (1+1+3) = $35.72

    Example 5:

    Place a sell order for 0.8 lots of XAGUSD on Monday and close the position on Wednesday: 

    SWAP =  1 × 0.8 × (2.8116) × (1+1) = $4.50

    Example 6:

    For example: USOUSD:  0.5 buy order on Monday and close the position on Wednesday:

    1 × 0.5 × (11.0718) × (1+1) = $11.07

    Overnight interest rate (percentage term)

    Formula: (Volume * Contract Size * Price)* interest rate/100/360 * number of days * exchange rate to USD

    Example 1:

    1 buy order on Monday and close the position on Wednesday

     

    SWAP =  (1*10*19867.4)*-9.1696/100/360 * (1+1) * 1USD) =-$101.21
