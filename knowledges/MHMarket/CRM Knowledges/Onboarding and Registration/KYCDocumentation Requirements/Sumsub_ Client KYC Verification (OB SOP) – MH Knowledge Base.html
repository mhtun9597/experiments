    Proof of identity (POI)

    The auditors must promptly and meticulously verify all identity documents submitted in KYC applications, ensuring that the verification of all documents is based on the established criteria.

    *Please refer following link: Qualified Proof of Identity (OB SOP)

     

    Proof of Address (POA)

    The process for reviewing Proof of Address (POA) is the same as for Proof of Identity (POI). The POA must meet the following requirements:

    Proof of Address (POA)

    • Documents must clearly display client's full name and complete residential address.
    • Current POA Requirements duration will be as below (based on Sumsub):

    POA – date of issue not older than 3 months

    1. Level 2
    2. Level 2 (after detecting no address on POI)

    POA – date of issue not older than 6 Months 

    1. Level 2
    2. MENA Level 1
    3. MENA EDD Check
    • The name on the document with client's POA must match the name on the client's submitted POI.

    • Currently, if a MAU client (excluding SVG client) submits a secondary identity document as proof of address (POA), even if it does not include an address, we can still accept it. For example, if a MAU client submits an ID card as proof of identity (POI) and then submits a passport as proof of address (POA), even if the passport does not include an address, we can still accept it.

      Option 1:

      1. ID document AND
      2. POA document

      E.g. UAE resident permit + Bank statement with full address

       

      Option 2:

      1. ID document AND
      2. Second ID document

      E.g. UAE resident permit + India passport

    Accepted Document Types:

    • Utility Bill (Electricity, Phone/Mobile, Water or Gas bill)
    • Statements from Financial Institutions (Bank, Credit Card, Insurance and Broker Statement)
    • Proof of Real Estate (Mortgage Statement or Certificate, Deed/Title deed, Ownership Certificate, Tenancy/Lease agreement)
    • Tax Letters & Notices (Tax Bill, Tax Notice)
    • Driving License (Must contain complete residential address and validity period of the license)
    • Documents and letters issued by government departments showing your full name and address. (Voters Registration Notice, Letters/notices issued by government housing authorities, Court Letters and Notices)

    Note - if client Request: Upload POI/POA on their behalf

    • Check if the sender’s email is registered and pending at the document stage.

    • If the document request is already in Sumsub, upload the document for the client and let Sumsub verify.

    Website clients: Require facial verification → ask them to follow the normal process.

    Exception: If registered via website by mistake and sale request for client it → change Sumsub to correct applicant level. Client can submit required POI/POA/Phone Verification (based on region) and may not need facial verification.

     

    If the "review status" in Sumsub indicates "Require Action," it means that manual review is needed.

    Process to review client's proof of identity (POI) & (POA)

    Step 1: First, click on the client's section.

     

    Step 2: After entering the following interface, we can check the client's review status under the "Event" section. This will display the reason why the KYC document is pending manual review.

     

    Step 3: Scroll down to the bottom to view the documents submitted by the customer. In position 3, you can see the reason why Sumsub did not pass the review.

    Step 4: Next, click on the ID/passport submitted by the client to determine whether it can be accepted.

     

    Step 5: After clicking, the client's ID photo will be displayed in a separate section.

     

    Step 6: There are 3 conditions that may be displayed.

    • Situation 1: If the client's KYC documents are acceptable, please follow the steps outlined below.
    • Situation 2: If the client's KYC documents do not meet the acceptance criteria, we need to reject them and ask the client to resubmit the documents.
    • Situation 3: Duplicate Account

    Situation 1: If the client's KYC documents are acceptable, please follow the steps outlined below.

    The process to review the client's ID

    Step 1: Click on "Return review" at position 6.

     

    Step 2: After clicking, the location marked as "2" in the image below will appear.

    Step 3: Next, click on the dark-colored indicator under "Bad photo" to change it to a lighter color.

     

    Step 4: Once all the indicators under "Bad photo" have changed to a lighter color, the image below will be displayed. At this point, click "Accept."

     

    Step 5: After clicking "Accept," the following image will be displayed.

     

    Step 6: Next, confirm that the correct side is selected.

    a: If the ID is front side, make sure select the "Front side."

    b: If the ID is back side, make sure select the "Back side."

     

    Step 7: Next, verify that the client's information is correctly filled out in the section below. If any information is incomplete, we need to assist in completing it based on the client ID.

     

    Step 8: Then, click on "Request check"

     

    Situation 2: If the client's KYC documents do not meet the acceptance criteria, we need to reject them and ask the client to resubmit the documents.

    Rejection Reason in Sumsub

    There are two parts of the rejection in Sumsub.

    1) Resubmission rejection reasons

     

    -When we select a rejection reason from here, the client can resubmit the required documents for us to review.

    -The "Review Status" will be changed to "Resubmission requested".

     

    2. Final rejection

     

    -When we select a rejection reason from here, the client will not be able to resubmit any documents. 

    -There are situations where we can change the review status to allow the client/IB to resubmit the KYC documents, and situations where it is not possible.

     

    i) The situations where we can change the review status:

    The documents which are submitted by the client in Sumsub are inconsistent with the personal information he/she filled in and appear to belong to different individuals.

    The Digital ID has been submitted too many times and rejected by Sumsub.

    If the IB/client requests to resubmit the KYC documents, we can reset the application to allow them to do so.

    The process for resetting the application.

    Step 1: Click on the icon with "three dots" in the top right corner of the following interface.

    Step 2: Next, select "Reset Application."

     

     

    ii) The situations where we cannot change the review status:

    If the client is rejected due to high risk factors, such as being a PEP or having a criminal record, we are unable to reset the application for the client.

    • Duplicate Account
    • Wrong Region
    • Forgery Document
    • Block List

     

    AML Screening

    -It is a type of identity verification process used to determine whether an individual is involved in money laundering activities.

    -AML screening will be triggered when the client fills out and submits their personal information during the registration process.

     

    -The following situation may arise:

    Situation 1: After completing the AML screening, if the "Watchlists" section appears as shown below, we can proceed with approval in Sumsub.

     

    Situation 2: If the "Watchlists" section appears as shown below, manual review is required.

    *If the client is flagged on the Watchlist, we must tag it as "Watchlist Checked."

    Step 1: Scroll to the "Watchlists" section and click the box under "Matches."

     

    Step 2: After clicking, the website's link where Sumsub has classified the client as an EDD (Enhanced Due Diligence) customer will be displayed. We need to review these links one by one to verify whether the individual described on the site is the client themselves.

     

    Step 3: After confirming, set the "is whitelisted" option to "On."

    Step 4: In the "Match status" option, select according to the following conditions:

    i) True positive: If the client's name, birthdate, and appearance on the website match completely, select "True positive."

    *If it's a true positive, we cannot accept this client and must reject them.

    pending pic

    ii) Potential positive: If the client's birthdate or appearance is inconsistent with the information on the website, select "Potential Positive."
    iii) False positive: If the customer's name, birthdate, and appearance are completely inconsistent on the website, select "False positive."

     

    EDD Questionnaire level

    -After ID AML Screening, if the watchlist show "strict match" as below, then the process will move to the EDD_Questionnaire level. The client will need to fill out the questionnaire and submit it in Sumsub. 

     

    -The 'Review Status' will be displayed below.

     

    Once the client submits the questionnaire, the Onboarding team will verify the submitted answers and proceed to approve the client. 

    The step to check the client's submitted questionnaire

    Step 1: Search for the 'Questionnaire' field in the above interface.

    Step 2: Press the position 2, then select "EDD"

    Step 3: Then select "EDD"

    Step 4: The questionnaire submitted by the client will be displayed as above. We need to review the answers, and there may be two possible situations.

    Situation 1: Once the client's submitted questionnaire is successful, we can proceed with approval in Sumsub.

    Situation 2: We need to review the answers, and if any of the responses are unsuitable, the client's application should be rejected. The sample answer below demonstrates a case where the application would be rejected.

    i) PEPcheck- Yes

    ii) Employmentcheck- Yes

    iii) declaration_of_screening_result- Yes

    iv) Final_Declaration- False

     

    -If client/sales submits a ticket stating that the client made a mistake in filling out the questionnaire, we need to reset the questionnaire and allow the client to resubmit it.

    The process to reset the questionnaire

    Step 1: Search for the 'Questionnaire' field in the above interface.

    Step 2: Press the position 2, then select "EDD"

    Step 3: Then select "EDD"

    Step 4: The questionnaire previously submitted by the client will be displayed above. Then, we click on the "Reset".

     

    Step 5: After the client resubmits the questionnaire, we need to review it. Once we confirm that the submitted questionnaire is correct, we can proceed with approval in Sumsub.

    Pending Case in Sumsub

    If we find that the case is pending review at the same level for too long (over 8-10 minutes), we need to seek assistance from the Sumsub team.

    Step to contact Sumsub team

    Step 1: Click on the "Help" icon in the upper-right corner.

    Step 2: Next, several options will appear. Please select "chat with us."

     

    Step 3: Then, the chat box will appear as shown above. Please select 'User Verification/Video Identification.

     

    Step 4: The bot will respond automatically. Then, type 'Agent' and select 'English' as the language.

     

    Step 5: Copy the client ID from Sumsub as shown below, describe the issue we're encountering, and send it to the Sumsub team for assistance in investigating the matter.

     

     

    Step 6: The Sumsub team usually takes 10-20 minutes to investigate the case. Once the issue is resolved, they will reply to inform us.

     

    Step 7: If there are no further questions, the ticket will be marked as resolved.

    *If the MU client is placed under "VU Client" /  If the VU client is placed under "MU Client" has been rejected:

    ii) Situation 2: Sales/Client request to resubmit.

    -Once confirmed with sales that the client needs to resubmit, we will proceed with changing the client's registered email and phone number in both CRM and Sumsub.

    Change the client's registered email and phone number in CRM

    Step 1: First, click on “Users” in the CRM system.

    Step 2: Under the “Users” option, click on “Account Opening Inquiry.”

    Step 3: After clicking, the following interface will appear. In number 3, choose the method to query the client, such as by client name or email.

    i) 3a : IB then select "IB Registration Account Opening Inquiry"

    ii) 3b : Client then select "TA Registration Account Opening Inquiry"

    Step 4: In number 4, choose the method to query the client, such as by client name or email, and then fill in the client information

    Step 5: Once completed, click on “Search.”

    Step 6: The corresponding IB/client information will be displayed. After confirming it’s correct, click the teal “+” icon.

    Step 7: In the “Operation” section, click on “Edit” icon.

    Step 8: The interface above will then pop up. Next, change the email address in the "Registered Mailbox" field.

    Example:

    Registered email: nojife6910@cantozil.com

    After changed: xxxnojife6910@cantozil.com

    Step 9: Next, we change the phone number in the "Registered Telephone Number" field.

    Example:

    Registered phone number: 0812345678

    After changed: xxx0812345678

    Step 10: After changing the email and phone number, then click on "Submit".

     

    Change the client's registered email and phone number in Sumsub

     

    Step 1: Find the IB/Client's registered email address in the "Applicant Information" section.

    Step 2: Next, change the email address.

    Example:

    Registered email: nojife6910@cantozil.com

    After changed: xxxnojife6910@cantozil.com

    Step 3: Then, click on "Save"

     

    Step 4: After that, find the IB/Client's registered phone number in the "Personal info" section.

    Step 5: Next, we change the phone number.

    Example:

    Registered phone number: 0812345678

    After changed: xxx0812345678

    Step 6: Then, click on "Save".

     

    -After changing client's registered email and phone number, the client may resubmit the documents.
